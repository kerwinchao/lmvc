1. Abstract

Zero-shot voice conversion (VC) converts source speech into any speaker's voice using only one utterance without model updates. Existing methods cannot generalize well to unseen speakers since limited speaker diversity in small speech data. Recently, language model-based methods have shown impressive results in zero-shot audio generation. Inspired by this, we attempt to explore the language model for zero-shot VC. However, taking the origin semantic and acoustic tokens from HuBERT and SoundStream as VC input, the language model has difficulty capturing content and speaker information from such lengthy and inadequately decoupled input. And the generation diversity of the language model may lead to unexpected outcomes during inference. To address these problems, we propose a language model-based VC (LM-VC), which is a two-stage language modeling technique that first generates coarse acoustic tokens that recover content and speaker identity, and reconstructs the fine for acoustic details. First, to enhance content maintenance and facilitates better disentanglement between content and speaker during coarse acoustic modeling, we utilize a masked prefix language model that employs a mask prediction strategy. This model is encouraged to recover masked content from the surrounding context and generate target speech from speaker timbre and corrupt content. Second, to further ensure speech generation stability, an external language model, which utilizes window attention to understand the local relation in acoustic space, is introduced to participate in coarse acoustic modeling with shallow fusion. Finally, a prefix language model reconstructs fine acoustic tokens from the coarse. Experiments on large-scale speech data show that LM-VC outperforms a language model-based baseline system on the zero-shot VC in terms of speech naturalness and speaker similarity.
